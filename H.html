Hydrogen Element

Overview

Hydrogen is a chemical element with the symbol H and atomic number 1. It is the lightest and most abundant element in the universe, making up about 75% of its elemental mass.

Properties

  • Atomic Number: 1
  • Symbol: H
  • Atomic Mass: 1.008 u
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Color: Colorless
  • Boiling Point: -252.87°C
  • Melting Point: -259.16°C
  • Density: 0.08988 g/L
  • Electronegativity: 2.20

Uses

Hydrogen has various applications, including being used as a fuel, in the production of ammonia for fertilizers, and in the petroleum and chemical industries.

Interesting Facts

  • Hydrogen is the primary fuel source for stars, including our sun.
  • It is the only element that can exist without neutrons.
  • Hydrogen is involved in the chemical reactions that produce water.
